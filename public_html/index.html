Abstract

Pittsburgh's bike sharing system Healthy Ride began the trail operation in Summer in 2015 and the ambition of the system is "expanding access to public transit through easy-to-use, affordable active transportation opportunities".This project aims to visualize the characteristics of th stations and patterns of rides from July 1st, 2015 to March 31st, 2016. The information of both the system average level and the specific station level is visualized. The visualization system will help both service provider and customers identify the features and dynamics embedded in Healthy Ride by the means of multiple visualization approaches.

On the system average level, the project visualizes the location of top five stations where the most bikes are rented and the variation of ride counts in the different circumstances within the different time granularity i.e., week, month or quarter. On the specific station level, the project visualizes the location of top five destination stations where the most bikes are returned when a specific station is set to be a departure point and the variation of ride counts in the different circumstances across the different weeks.
